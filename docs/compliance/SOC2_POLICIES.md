## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose

This document defines the comprehensive SOC 2 Type II policy framework for VantisOS, establishing policies across all five Trust Services Criteria (TSC).

### 1.2 Scope

These policies apply to:
- All VantisOS components and subsystems
- All employees, contractors, and third parties
- All data processed, stored, or transmitted by VantisOS
- All security and compliance activities

### 1.3 Policy Principles

All policies are based on the following principles:
- **Security First**: Security is a fundamental requirement
- **Compliance**: All policies comply with applicable laws and regulations
- **Continuous Improvement**: Policies are regularly reviewed and updated
- **Accountability**: Clear roles and responsibilities are defined
- **Transparency**: Policies are communicated and accessible

## 2. Security Policies

### 2.1 Access Control Policy

#### Policy Statement

VantisOS implements a comprehensive access control policy to ensure that only authorized individuals have access to system resources and data.

#### Policy Requirements

**2.1.1 Access Control Principles**

- Access is granted based on the principle of least privilege
- Access is granted based on business need
- Access is regularly reviewed and updated
- Access is revoked when no longer required

**2.1.2 Access Control Mechanisms**

- Multi-factor authentication is required for all access
- Role-based access control (RBAC) is implemented
- Capability-based access control is used for system resources
- Access is logged and monitored

**2.1.3 Access Control Procedures**

- Access requests must be approved by authorized personnel
- Access must be granted within 24 hours of approval
- Access must be reviewed quarterly
- Access must be revoked within 24 hours of termination

#### Policy Enforcement

- Access control violations are immediately reported
- Access control violations are investigated
- Access control violations result in disciplinary action
- Access control violations are documented

### 2.2 System Monitoring Policy

#### Policy Requirements

- All security events are monitored

**2.2.2 Monitoring Capabilities**

- Real-time monitoring is implemented
- Anomaly detection is implemented
- Alerting and notification is implemented
- Log aggregation and analysis is implemented

**2.2.3 Monitoring Procedures**

- Monitoring is continuous (24/7)
- Alerts are investigated within 15 minutes
- Critical alerts are escalated immediately
- Monitoring logs are retained for 2 years

#### Policy Enforcement

- Monitoring failures are immediately reported
- Monitoring failures are investigated
- Monitoring failures result in corrective action
- Monitoring failures are documented

## 7. Policy Management

### 7.1 Policy Development

#### Policy Development Process

1. **Policy Identification**: Identify need for new or updated policy
2. **Policy Drafting**: Draft policy with clear requirements
3. **Policy Review**: Review policy with stakeholders
4. **Policy Approval**: Approve policy through formal process
5. **Policy Publication**: Publish policy to appropriate audience

### 7.2 Policy Communication

#### Policy Communication Process

1. **Policy Announcement**: Announce new or updated policy
2. **Policy Training**: Provide training on policy requirements
3. **Policy Distribution**: Distribute policy to affected parties
4. **Policy Acknowledgment**: Obtain acknowledgment of policy

### 7.3 Policy Enforcement

#### Policy Enforcement Process

1. **Policy Monitoring**: Monitor compliance with policy
2. **Policy Auditing**: Audit compliance with policy
3. **Policy Violation Handling**: Handle policy violations appropriately
4. **Policy Improvement**: Improve policy based on feedback

### 7.4 Policy Review and Update

#### Policy Review Process

1. **Policy Review Schedule**: Review policies annually
2. **Policy Review Criteria**: Review based on changes in requirements
3. **Policy Update Process**: Update policies as needed
4. **Policy Version Control**: Maintain version control of policies
