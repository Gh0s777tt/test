# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our
community include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes,
  and learning from the experience
* Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the
  overall community

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or
  advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address,
  without their explicit permission
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when
an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.
Examples of representing our community include using an official e-mail address,
posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed
representative at an online or offline event.

## Reporting

If you believe someone is violating the Code of Conduct, please report it by
sending an email to [conduct@vantisos.org](mailto:conduct@vantisos.org).

All reports will be reviewed and investigated by the moderation team and will
result in a response that is deemed necessary and appropriate to the
circumstances. The moderation team pledges to maintain the confidentiality
with regard to the reporter of an incident.

For more details, see our [Reporting Guidelines](#reporting-guidelines) below.

## Moderation

### Moderation Team

The current moderation team for VantisOS is:

| Name | GitHub | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| Vantis Admin | @vantisAdmin | Lead Moderator |
| Vantis Security | @vantisSecurity | Security & Compliance |
| Vantis Community | @vantisCommunity | Community Manager |

### Moderator Responsibilities

Moderators are expected to:

1. **Review reports promptly**: All reports should be acknowledged within 24 hours and resolved within 7 days
2. **Maintain confidentiality**: Reporter identities will be kept confidential unless required for investigation
3. **Apply sanctions fairly**: Sanctions will be based on the severity of the violation and prior history
4. **Document all actions**: All moderation actions will be documented for transparency
5. **Escalate serious violations**: Legal threats, harassment, or doxxing will be escalated to the full moderation team

### Sanctions

Moderators may apply any of the following sanctions based on the severity of the violation:

| Violation Type | First Offense | Second Offense | Third Offense |
|----------------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| Minor (unintentional) | Warning | Temporary suspension (1-7 days) | Suspension (30 days) |
| Moderate (intentional) | Warning + required edit | Temporary suspension (7-14 days) | Permanent ban |
| Severe (harassment, threats) | Temporary suspension (14-30 days) | Permanent ban | N/A |
| Critical (illegal, doxxing) | Permanent ban | N/A | N/A |

### Appeal Process

If you believe a moderation action was taken in error:

1. Send an appeal to [appeal@vantisos.org](mailto:appeal@vantisos.org) within 14 days of the action
2. Clearly explain why you believe the action was incorrect
3. Provide any relevant evidence or context
4. Appeals will be reviewed by a different moderator who was not involved in the original decision
5. You will receive a response within 14 days

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

**Via Email:**
- Send your report to [conduct@vantisos.org](mailto:conduct@vantisos.org)
- Include "CoC Report" in the subject line
- All reports are confidential

**What to Include:**
1. Your contact information (optional, but recommended for follow-up)
2. Name(s) of the person(s) involved
3. Description of the incident with timestamps if possible
4. Any relevant screenshots, logs, or links
5. Names of any witnesses (if applicable)

### What Happens Next

1. **Acknowledgment**: You'll receive an acknowledgment within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: The moderation team will investigate the incident
3. **Action**: Appropriate action will be taken based on the severity
4. **Follow-up**: You'll be notified of the outcome (confidential details will be redacted)
5. **Support**: If you need support during or after the process, let us know

### Confidentiality

All reports are treated with strict confidentiality. Reporter identities are only shared:
- With the moderation team
- When required for a thorough investigation
- When required by law

We will never share your identity with the person you're reporting unless absolutely necessary.

## Additional Guidelines

### VantisOS-Specific Guidelines

As a formally verified operating system project, we have additional community guidelines:

1. **Formal Verification**: Discussions about formal verification, proofs, and security properties should be technical and constructive
2. **Security**: Security vulnerabilities should be reported responsibly through our security policy (see SECURITY.md)
3. **Architecture**: Architecture discussions should be evidence-based and reference our ADR (Architecture Decision Records)
4. **Testing**: All claims about correctness, performance, or security should be accompanied by tests or benchmarks
5. **Documentation**: Documentation improvements are always welcome and highly valued

### Inclusive Language

We encourage the use of inclusive language in all communications:

* Avoid gendered pronouns when the gender is unknown (use "they" instead of "he/she")
* Avoid ableist language (e.g., "crazy", "insane", "lame")
* Avoid culturally appropriative language
* Use person-first language (e.g., "people with disabilities" instead of "disabled people")

### Conflict Resolution

If you have a conflict with another community member:

1. **Try to resolve it directly**: First, attempt to resolve the conflict privately and respectfully
2. **Request mediation**: If direct resolution fails, you can request mediation from a moderator
3. **Report if necessary**: If the conflict involves harassment or violations of this CoC, report it
